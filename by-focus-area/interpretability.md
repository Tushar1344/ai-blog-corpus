### Tracing Model Outputs to the Training Data

- **ID:** `ant-r-influence-functions`
- **Company:** Anthropic
- **Link:** https://www.anthropic.com/research/influence-functions
- **Date:** 2023-08-08
- **Track:** research
- **Contribution type:** empirical-study
- **Signal:** H — L=H/N=M/A=M (priority 7.7)

**Summary:**

- Scales influence functions (a classical stat technique) to LLMs up to 52B parameters to identify which training examples most shape a given output
- Uses efficient inverse-Hessian-vector product approximations and gradient-of-candidate-examples to avoid prohibitive retraining costs
- Finds generalization becomes more abstract with scale — 52B model surfaces conceptually related examples rather than token overlaps
- Cross-lingual influence strengthens with scale
- influence distribution is power-law but diffuse (not pure memorization)
- Top-down complement to mechanistic interpretability
- influence can also be localized within network layers.

### Unsupervised sentiment neuron

- **ID:** `oai-r-unsupervised-sentiment-neuron`
- **Company:** OpenAI
- **Link:** https://openai.com/index/unsupervised-sentiment-neuron/
- **Date:** _date unknown_
- **Track:** research
- **Contribution type:** interpretability-finding
- **Signal:** M — L=M/N=M/A=L (priority 5.6)

**Summary:**

- Discovered single 'sentiment neuron' emerging from a char-level mLSTM trained on 82M Amazon reviews
- Linear classifier on the representation hits 91.8% on Stanford Sentiment Treebank (SOTA at time)
